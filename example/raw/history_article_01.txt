The Renaissance was a period of great cultural and intellectual growth in Europe, spanning roughly from the 14th to the 17th century. It began in Italy and spread throughout the continent, influencing art, science, literature, and philosophy. Artists like Leonardo da Vinci and Michelangelo created masterpieces that are still admired today. Scientists such as Galileo and Copernicus challenged traditional views of the universe, laying the groundwork for modern science. Writers like Shakespeare and Cervantes explored the complexities of human nature. The Renaissance marked a shift from medieval to modern thinking, emphasizing individual achievement and the pursuit of knowledge.
